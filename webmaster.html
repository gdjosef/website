How'd you get into NeoCities?

I got into NeoCities trying to find a website to make a road sign generator. After being bored for a bit, I ran across a NeoCities link and what led to it was sparkles, imood statuses, autoplaying music, and GIFs galore. Unfortunately, for the life of me, I can't find it anymore due to me switching Firefox profiles and the browser history being like drowned out by 4 months worth of web browsing.

Although even before that, I was always fascinated by Japanese ISP websites. Which sound exactly like what they are. Even if I couldn't read them by heart, seeing all the images and Japanese text was really charming imagining someone just updating their new blog for all their friends to see, whoever they might be.

How'd you learn HTML/CSS/JS?

As opposed to a majority of users on this site, I did not infact learn HTML/CSS for the purpose of making websites on here! Before this, I was super into making Discord bots. DEFINITELY NOT PRODUCTION READY, but learning the aspects of JavaScript one by one was fun! Of course, none of that was HTML/CSS because Node.js stuff. That was my kickstart with JavaScript basically. I continued with a Codecademy course and the rest is history.

My HTML/CSS knowledge started out mostly from little tidbits of information from my curiosity around the web although I gradually went into MDN's Getting started with the web guide to expand my knowledge more. Do recommend. I've also seen w3schools thrown out a lot. Not enough experience to recommend or not but what I will say is that its references are much more lackluster than MDNs. The tutorials might do the job but for documentation, MDN is the way to go.

I made josef's neocities zone (and all of it's derivatives, rest in peace the really early ones, most were just nostalgiamining anyway) as a way to hone my skills in HTML/CSS whilst making a real neat personal page for myself. What do you use to write/code your website?

Visual Studio Code! I sort of don't care that much about VSCode's telemetry, its probably* going somewhere good anyway. VSCodium if you care enough about privacy to warrant needing it. Exact same, but just built from source w/o telemetry to Microsoft.

As much as I'm retroactively nostalgic for the old web, I will forever be loyal to much more fleshed out code editors. Fair enough if you like making a website like this using just Notepad to emulate that *90s* feel (just use Dreamweaver) but I seriously cannot live without it since I've basically been making websites even before NeoCities.

* emphasis on probably

Influences on your site design?
